Why Traditional SaaS Pricing Doesn't Fit

The philosophical and strategic reasons we reject per-spend models

The Core Problem

Traditional SaaS pricing models — per-user, per-seat, per-data-unit — are based on a simple assumption: as the customer gets more value from the product, they use more of it, so they should pay more.

But brandhub inverts this. We save clients money. Charging them more when they spend more is a philosophical contradiction. It's also a perverse incentive.

The Trap We Won't Fall Into: Competitors charge 15–19% of POS spend as platform tolling fees. This creates an incentive for the platform to maximize client spending, not minimize waste. It's a conflict of interest baked into the pricing model. We reject this entirely.

What Drives Actual Value

✓
Intelligence: Better data means better decisions. The client benefits from insights we provide, regardless of whether they spend $100K or $1M through the platform.
✓
Efficiency: The platform's workflow and integration cuts client time waste. This saves money. The benefit is real even if they never increase spend.
✓
Network access: Buyers have access to a curated, pre-vetted vendor network. That saves search time and reduces decision risk. It's valuable at any spend level.

Why "Users," "Items," and "Programs" Aren't Good Proxies

Per-User Pricing

A client adds one additional user to the platform. The platform doesn't get more valuable; the user gets more value from accessing the same data set. This is asymmetric. The client shouldn't pay more for a feature that benefits only them.

Per-Item Pricing

A client adds 1,000 more POS items to the platform. The platform becomes more valuable for everyone (more inventory, more data, more opportunities for insights). The client that added the items benefits but so does everyone else. They shouldn't be taxed for contributing value.

Per-Program Pricing

A client runs two marketing programs instead of one. The platform cost shouldn't scale with marketing volume. That's arbitrary.

Our Pricing Principle

"We charge for access to the platform and the intelligence it provides. Not for how much you spend through it."

Flat annual fee. All-inclusive. No hidden scaling. One price, one conversation, one contract.

Long-Term: The Marketplace Model

What we don't share with clients yet — the monetization architecture

What brandhub Actually Is

brandhub is not just a procurement tool. It's the pipe through which business flows between brands and suppliers. That makes it a two-sided marketplace, and two-sided marketplaces have fundamentally different monetization logic than simple SaaS platforms.

The value isn't in the software interface. The value is in the business that flows through it. The platform only gets better as more participants join. That network effect is the moat.

Brand Side: Flat Annual Fee

Brands pay one flat annual fee for access to the platform, the vendor network, and the intelligence we provide.

→
Not tied to spend: $500K or $5M in annual POS spending through Select, brandhub, or others. Same fee.
→
Predictable: One price, one year, one contract. Easy to renew.
→
Scales simply: As we add brands, the cost to serve an additional brand is incremental. Margin improves without touching pricing.

Vendor Side: Participation Fees

Vendors (like Select, but also Branded Collateral, AMPAC, and others) pay for access to qualified demand on the platform. The model has several components:

1. Listing / Presence Fee (Small Annual)

Vendor gets presence in the brandhub network, ability to see and respond to RFQs from brands on the platform.

2. Per-RFQ Participation Fee OR Revenue Share

Two models, vendor chooses:

Option A (Transaction-based): Vendor pays 1–2% of value on each RFQ they win and book through brandhub.

Option B (Participation-based): Vendor pays per RFQ they bid on, or pays a monthly participation tier fee.

3. Critical Carve-Out: Existing Vendor Relationships

Important: Any vendors a brand brings with them to brandhub are NOT charged. They can participate at zero cost. Only net-new vendor relationships facilitated by brandhub pay the participation fee.

Why? Because this removes a friction point. Brands worry about bringing their existing vendors onto a new platform if those vendors have to pay a toll. By exempting existing relationships, we remove that concern and make adoption easy. The network fee applies only to incremental opportunity on the platform.

This is standard B2B marketplace practice. Faire does it. Angi does it. It works.

The Flywheel Effect

More brands join

↓

More RFQ volume on the platform

↓

Platform becomes more valuable to vendors

↓

More vendors join, willing to pay for access

↓

Vendor fee revenue subsidizes lower brand fees

↓

Easier to sign brands, which accelerates the cycle

This is a defensible network effect. The longer we're in market, the harder we are to displace, not the other way around.

Why We Don't Lead With This

We don't tell brands about the vendor participation fees in year one. Here's why:

1.
It complicates the conversation. The first sale is about simplicity: "One fee, all-in, you get the platform and the network."
2.
The vendor model doesn't generate meaningful revenue until we have volume (50+ vendors + strong RFQ flow). Right now, it's infrastructure building.
3.
We want brands thinking about their own benefit, not about how the platform is monetizing vendors. That comes later, after trust is built.

But internally, we're building toward this. The vendor network is the long-term moat.

The Select + brandhub Relationship

Be transparent, not defensive, about the competitive dynamics

The Reality

Select built brandhub. Select is one vendor in the network. Select competes for business like every other vendor—but Select has real, disclosed advantages that deserve acknowledgment.

The secret to sustainable marketplace monetization is honesty about conflicts of interest, not hiding them.

Select's Competitive Advantages in brandhub

1.
Speed: Select's systems are integrated into brandhub's core. Turnaround time on RFQs is faster.
2.
Integration: Brands get visibility into Select's production pipeline directly from brandhub. That's valuable and not available to competitors (yet).
3.
Industry expertise: Select's team has 20+ years in alcohol marketing. We understand the nuances. That's earned, not given.

How We Formalize This Without Hiding It

The Production Credit Model (Disclosed)

At $400K+ annual Select spend, brands receive a $12K credit toward brandhub fees. At $800K+, they receive $24K. This is transparent. It's on the contract. It's the client benefit for using Select.

What This Does

It acknowledges Select's advantage explicitly, translates that advantage into a price benefit for the client (not a hidden profit margin for Select), and makes the economics clear to both parties. No backdoor deals. No confusion.

What the GT Study Found

"Select Design's ability to combine industry and brand expertise with a highly customizable platform is a key differentiator in the market."

This is our moat. It's not just about having better software. It's about having people who understand the business, combined with flexible technology. Don't hide that. Lead with it.

The Conversation With Brands

Here's how we address it directly:

"We built brandhub. We're also a vendor on it. Yes, we have advantages—speed, integration, and industry knowledge. We don't hide that. Instead, we make it a client benefit through the production credit. Your spend with us directly reduces your brandhub fee. That keeps us honest: we succeed when you succeed, not when we extract rent from the platform."

Brands respect transparency. They know marketplaces have conflicts. They prefer vendors who acknowledge it and design systems to manage it, rather than vendors who pretend it doesn't exist.

Pricing Principles (The Rules We Won't Break)

These are non-negotiable. They define our integrity in the market.

No % of Spend

We don't profit from your waste. If you spend $100K or $1M through the platform, your fee is the same. We're incentivized to help you spend smarter, not more.

No Per-Seat Scaling

More users on brandhub means more value for you. They shouldn't cost you more. Usage isn't a cost driver; it's a benefit indicator.

No Per-Item Fees

Adding SKUs to the platform makes it better for everyone. The client contributes to the network. They shouldn't be taxed for participation.

No Per-Program Costs

Running two marketing programs instead of one shouldn't double your platform fee. The platform supports unlimited programs for one price.

Vendor Network Fees Disclosed

If we charge vendors for participation, brands know about it. No hidden economics. Transparency builds trust and prevents future friction.

Founding Client Rates Locked

Sign in 2026 at $42K/year? You stay at $42K for three years. No surprise escalations. Price certainty is part of the value prop.

Why These Principles Matter

💡

These principles define us. They separate us from competitors who charge 15–19% of POS spend and have incentives to maximize volume, not minimize waste.

In a fragmented market with low satisfaction, pricing integrity is a competitive advantage. Brands remember which vendors they can trust.
